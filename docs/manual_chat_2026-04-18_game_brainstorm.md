## Finding 1 — Slow to land in the brainstorm register on a creative-first opener

**What happened:** Walter walked in saying *"I want to make a game and I have an idea, but I don't know if it'll work."* The orchestrator routed to Realist three turns in a row, each of which gated the conversation on *"is this a business or a passion project? do you need this to make money?"* before any creative exploration could happen. Even after Walter said "I'd actually just like to brainstorm around the idea," it took an explicit user redirect to get Creative summoned.

When Creative engaged, the brainstorm worked beautifully — generative questions about emotional core, constraint design, failure-vs-discovery framing. That's the panel doing exactly what the user wanted. **Realist's three turns were not wrong** in the abstract — "is this a business or a passion project" is a real and fair question, and a friend asking it would not be out of line. The tuning observation is the **3-round delay** to land in the register Walter actually came for. On a creative-first opener, leading with the business-validation gate makes the conversation feel transactional before it has a chance to feel exploratory.

**Why this is a product issue, not a Walter issue:** the system pushed business-framing because Realist's `description_for_orchestrator` (correctly) pulls in when financial reality isn't named. But for a creative cold-open, *no specialist's description marks them as the right voice for "let's explore an idea before we even know if it's a business."* Creative's description says it's most useful in *exploration (finding the angle) and synthesis (giving the refined idea its shape)* — but the orchestrator read "make a game" as "ambiguous business intent" and routed to Realist for grounding instead.

This connects directly to a parked consideration in [BUILD.md Phase 7](../BUILD.md#parked-considerations-not-scheduled--revisit-when-relevant):

> **Ideation vs Creative/Designer — "brainstorm" register? (raised 2026-04-18).** Ideation (7.7) is scoped narrowly as a cold-open host. But there's a separate thought worth holding: what happens when a user walks in with *"I have five business ideas and I need help picking one"* or *"what game should I create"*? That's a real ideation *process* inside the conversation — not orientation.

This manual session is **direct field evidence** that the parked consideration is real. The current Creative voice handles the brainstorm register well *once it's summoned* — what's missing is the routing pull that gets it summoned without the user having to say "I'd actually just like to brainstorm." That's an orchestrator-prompt change (probably a "when the user comes in with an idea/concept and hasn't framed it as a business problem, lead with Creative or Designer for at least one round before Realist gates on financial framing"), not a new specialist.

Suggested follow-up cycle (after Realist replication lands): a **brainstorm-register routing edit** to the orchestrator prompt, plus possibly a Creative description tightening to make it a stronger pull on creative-first openers. Validate with a new persona — `creative_first_opener.json` — that walks in with a game/product/concept idea and explicitly does *not* mention business or revenue.

There's a soft GR#7 echo here too: the panel had a default arc ("answer the business question first") and the user had to redirect to escape it. Not a violation — Realist's questions were real questions — but the kind of quiet pattern GR#7 was named to make visible. A creative-first opener should be allowed to stay creative for a turn or two before financial gating arrives.

## Finding 2 — Creative was truncated mid-sentence three times (token cap, not interrupt)

**What happened:** Creative's first three brainstorming turns each ended mid-sentence with no completion:
- Cutoff 1: *"Is it the joy of chaos—"* (em-dash, mid-thought)
- Cutoff 2: *"...because I have to figure"* (mid-word — sentence not finished)
- Cutoff 3: *"If it's discovery — if being stranded"* (mid-clause)

After Walter asked Creative to repeat (twice), the recovered turns were shorter and completed cleanly.

**Root cause (confirmed):** [lib/agents/token-budgets.ts](../lib/agents/token-budgets.ts) sets `creative: 220` tokens (≈ 165 words) under Phase 7.4's length-compression rationale. The cutoff turns above ran roughly 175-200 words — right at and above the cap. The model is producing a longer response than the cap allows, and the LLM client truncates wherever the token budget runs out — which is mid-sentence or mid-word, with no graceful completion.

Walter was *not* clicking, *not* interrupting, *not* doing anything other than waiting for the stream to finish. The orchestrator note on the third Creative turn says *"Creative was mid-sentence when you interrupted"* — that note is **incorrect**; the truncation happened with no interrupt event. (Worth checking: does the orchestrator's "you interrupted" framing fire whenever a previous agent message looks incomplete? If so, the orchestrator is mis-attributing token-budget truncation to user interrupts. Routing-reason text is product surface — it should not lie about what happened.)

**Compounding issue:** the model doesn't know it has only 220 tokens. It writes a response sized for an unconstrained turn, then gets sliced at the boundary. Two real fixes:
1. **Raise Creative's cap for brainstorm turns** — Creative's role in brainstorming is genuinely longer than its role in synthesis. A flat 220 cap fights the brainstorm register. Either bump to ~350 (matching Realist's quantitative-reasoning band) or build a context-aware cap that reads the deliberation phase.
2. **Tell the model its budget** — add an explicit sentence to the system prompt: *"Your turn is capped at roughly 165 words. Plan your response to land cleanly within that budget."* The model can self-edit when it knows the constraint.

Both fixes belong in the brainstorm-register cycle, paired with Finding 1.

**Secondary issue:** the orchestrator's routing-reason text on the third turn falsely claimed the user had interrupted. That's a product-surface correctness bug — the orchestrator is reading downstream state (a truncated agent message) and inferring "must have been interrupted." Worth fixing whenever the orchestrator prompt is next touched: don't claim interrupt unless the harness/UI actually emitted an interrupt event.
