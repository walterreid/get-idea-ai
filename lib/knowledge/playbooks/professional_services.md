# Professional Services (Lawyers, Therapists, Consultants, Accountants)

## What Actually Converts

### 1. Google Search Ads (Specialty + Location)
The highest-ROI channel for professional services — but only with narrow targeting. "Divorce lawyer [city]" converts. "Lawyer" doesn't. The more specific the query, the higher the intent: "ADHD testing Greenwich CT" outperforms "therapist near me" by 5-8x on conversion rate. Bid on the long-tail specialty + location combinations.

**Why it works:** Professional services are high-consideration, high-intent purchases. People searching are ready to hire — they just need to find the right person. The cost-per-click is high ($15-80 depending on practice area) but the value-per-client justifies it: one personal injury case, one therapy client with insurance, one tax client with a business is worth $2,000-50,000+.

**Budget guidance:** Minimum viable spend is $1,000/month with tight keyword matching. Below that, the data is too thin to optimize. At $2,000-3,000/month, you can cover your top 3-5 specialty terms with enough volume to learn what converts.

### 2. Google Business Profile (GBP)
Critical for therapists, dentists, and local attorneys. Less important for consultants who work nationally. A therapist in a therapist-dense zip code MUST have a GBP that's complete, has 30+ reviews, and lists every specialty they treat. The local 3-pack for "anxiety therapist [neighborhood]" is free traffic from people ready to book.

**The directory problem:** Professional services have the worst directory fragmentation. A therapist might be on Psychology Today, GoodTherapy, Zocdoc, and their insurance panel — all with different addresses, phone numbers, or specialties listed. If they moved in the last 2 years, their old address is probably still on half these listings. Fix this first — it's free and it matters more than any paid channel.

### 3. LinkedIn (Organic Thought Leadership)
The one category where LinkedIn organic actually justifies the time — for B2B professionals (consultants, accountants, business attorneys). Not for therapists. Not for personal injury. LinkedIn works when the decision-maker is also on LinkedIn: CFOs hiring accountants, VPs hiring consultants, GCs hiring corporate attorneys.

**What works:** 2-3 posts per week with specific, opinionated insights from real client work (anonymized). Not articles — short posts. Not "thought leadership" — judgment calls. Share the non-obvious thing you told a client this week.

## What Wastes Money

**Instagram/Facebook for most professional services:** Lawyers, therapists, and accountants get sold on "building a brand presence" on social. The audience isn't there for these services. Exception: family law attorneys in smaller markets where community presence matters, and cosmetic practitioners (dermatology, plastic surgery) where visuals drive consideration.

**SEO as the primary strategy for new practices:** A new therapist competing against Psychology Today, WebMD, and established practices for "therapist near me" is going to wait 12-18 months to see page-one results. Search Ads get immediate visibility while organic builds in the background.

**Podcast sponsorships / brand campaigns:** Professional services convert on search intent, not awareness. A consultant spending $1,500/month sponsoring a podcast is building someone else's audience, not their own pipeline. Invest that same amount in Google Ads for "outsourced CFO [city]" and count the leads.

**Generic website redesigns without conversion strategy:** A beautiful website that doesn't have a clear intake form, a prominent phone number, and specialty pages for each service area is a brochure, not a lead generator. Fix the conversion architecture before spending $10K on a redesign.

## Budget Tiers

- **$500/mo:** All Google Search Ads — tight keyword list (top 3 specialty + location terms), conversion tracking on form fills and calls. No split. Learn what converts before expanding.
- **$2,000/mo:** Google Search Ads ($1,200) + GBP optimization and review strategy ($300) + directory audit and cleanup ($200) + LinkedIn content strategy for B2B only ($300).
- **$5,000/mo:** Google Search Ads ($2,500) + GBP management ($400) + content marketing for SEO long-game ($1,000) + LinkedIn Ads for B2B professionals ($800) + retargeting past website visitors ($300).

## The One Mistake Everyone Makes

**Trying to rank for the generic term instead of owning the specialty.** "Therapist near me" has 1,000 competitors. "ADHD testing [specific suburb]" has 3. "Business lawyer" is unwinnable at $500/month. "Franchise agreement attorney [city]" is wide open. The plan should identify the 3-5 specialty + location combinations where this practice can realistically own page one — and ignore everything else until those are won.

## When Research Is Thin

If research finds little about the practice, check for: (1) a Psychology Today or Avvo profile that ranks above their website — common, and it means their own site is underperforming; (2) no Google reviews — nearly guaranteed for practices under 2 years old; (3) a website that lists services generically instead of by specialty. The diagnosis is usually "invisible for the searches that matter most" rather than "no online presence at all." These professionals exist online — they just exist in the wrong places for the searches their ideal clients are making.
