# E-commerce & DTC (Online Stores, Shopify, Product Brands)

## What Actually Converts

### 1. Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)
For e-commerce, Meta is the primary acquisition channel and it's not close. Unlike local services, product purchases ARE impulsive — someone scrolling Instagram can see a product, click, and buy in 90 seconds. Meta's targeting for purchase behavior is the strongest in the industry, and the visual ad format (video, carousel, collection) is built for product discovery.

**What works:** Video-first creative showing the product in use. Not lifestyle branding — demonstration. UGC (user-generated content) style ads outperform polished brand ads by 2-3x on ROAS for DTC brands under $5M revenue. Test 3-5 creative variants per week. Kill losers at 3-day mark. Scale winners by 20% increments.

**Budget guidance:** Meta needs volume to optimize. Below $50/day, the algorithm doesn't have enough data to find your audience. $100/day ($3,000/month) is the minimum viable budget for a single product. Below that, you're paying Meta's learning tax without getting the optimization payoff.

### 2. Google Shopping + Search
The second pillar. Meta drives discovery ("I didn't know I needed this"); Google captures intent ("I'm looking for [product category]"). Google Shopping Ads show the product, price, and image directly in search results — high intent, high conversion. Standard Search Ads complement Shopping for branded terms and specific product queries.

**The feed matters more than the bid:** Product titles, descriptions, and images in your Google Merchant Center feed determine where your Shopping Ads appear. "Organic face serum" won't show for "vitamin C serum for acne scars." Optimize the feed before increasing spend.

### 3. Email & SMS (Retention Engine)
The cheapest revenue channel by far, and most DTC brands underinvest catastrophically. Email/SMS should generate 25-40% of total revenue for a healthy DTC brand. Flows to build first: welcome series (5 emails), abandoned cart (3 emails + 1 SMS), post-purchase (review request + cross-sell), and win-back (90-day lapsed).

**The math:** A 10,000-person email list generating $2/subscriber/month = $20,000/month in revenue at near-zero marginal cost. Compare that to paying $30-50 per new customer through Meta.

## What Wastes Money

**Influencer marketing without tracking:** "Send free product, hope for sales" is not a strategy. If you can't attribute revenue to each influencer (unique discount codes, UTM links), you can't optimize. Most micro-influencer campaigns generate brand awareness that feels good but doesn't move the P&L. The exception: affiliates with commission-based compensation, where alignment is structural.

**SEO as a primary strategy for new brands:** A 6-month-old Shopify store competing for "best face serum" against Sephora, Allure, and Wirecutter is going to lose. SEO is a 12-18 month play that works after you have brand recognition, reviews, and backlinks. Start with paid acquisition, build the email list, then invest in content/SEO once you have revenue to fund it.

**TikTok Ads (premature):** TikTok's algorithm is incredible for organic virality but the ad platform is still immature compared to Meta. The CPMs are lower but the conversion tracking is unreliable, and the audience skews younger (lower purchasing power for most DTC categories). Organic TikTok content? Yes. Paid TikTok budget before Meta is profitable? No.

**Amazon Ads without margin analysis:** If your product margin after Amazon fees is 25%, spending 15% on Amazon PPC leaves 10% profit — before returns, customer service, and overhead. Many DTC brands are profitable on their own site and unprofitable on Amazon but don't realize it because they're looking at revenue, not contribution margin.

## Budget Tiers

- **$500/mo:** Not enough for meaningful paid acquisition. Focus entirely on email/SMS setup (welcome + abandoned cart flows), organic social content (3-5 posts/week), and optimizing the conversion rate on your site. Every 0.1% improvement in conversion rate is worth more than $500 in ads.
- **$2,000/mo:** Meta Ads ($1,200) + Google Shopping ($500) + email/SMS platform ($300). This is minimum viable paid. Run 2-3 ad creatives, test audiences, and measure ROAS weekly.
- **$5,000/mo:** Meta Ads ($2,500) + Google Shopping + Search ($1,200) + email/SMS with advanced flows ($400) + UGC content creation for ad creative ($500) + retargeting ($400). This is the level where Meta's algorithm starts working for you.

## The One Mistake Everyone Makes

**Spending on acquisition before the site converts.** A Shopify store with a 1% conversion rate is leaking 99 out of every 100 visitors. Doubling ad spend doubles the leak. Before spending a dollar on ads: fix the product pages (real customer photos, specific benefit copy, not feature lists), add social proof (reviews above the fold), simplify checkout (guest checkout, fewer form fields), and get abandoned cart emails running. A 0.5% improvement in conversion rate is worth more than a 50% increase in ad budget.

## When Research Is Thin

If the store has minimal presence, the diagnosis is usually one of: (1) they're pre-launch and need a launch strategy, not a marketing plan; (2) they're selling on Amazon/Etsy and have no owned channel (website + email list); or (3) they have a Shopify store with traffic but can't figure out why nobody buys. Check their site for: product page quality, review count, pricing relative to competitors, and whether the value proposition is clear in 5 seconds. The marketing plan is only useful after the store's conversion fundamentals are sound.
