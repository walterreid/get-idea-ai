# Local Services (HVAC, Plumbing, Landscaping, Electricians)

## What Actually Converts

### 1. Google Local Services Ads (LSAs)
The single highest-converting channel for home services. LSAs appear above standard search ads, show the Google Guaranteed badge, and charge per lead — not per click. A plumber paying $25/lead through LSAs will outperform one paying $15/click on standard Search where half the clicks are tire-kickers.

**Why it works:** Homeowners searching "emergency plumber near me" at 10pm are not browsing. They're buying. LSAs match that intent with a trust signal (Google Guaranteed) and a phone number. Conversion rates run 15-25% from lead to booked job — 3-5x better than standard Search Ads for the same queries.

**Budget guidance:** $500/mo gets 15-25 leads depending on trade and market. $2,000/mo saturates most local markets for a single trade. Don't spend $5,000/mo on LSAs unless you're multi-trade or multi-location — you'll hit the ceiling and start paying for lower-quality leads.

### 2. Google Business Profile (GBP)
The most underinvested channel in home services. A complete, active GBP with 50+ reviews and weekly posts will appear in the local 3-pack for dozens of searches you'd pay $8-15/click for in Search Ads.

**What "active" means:** Post job photos weekly. Respond to every review within 24 hours. Update service areas quarterly. Add seasonal services. The owners who treat GBP like a living presence beat the ones who set it and forget it — Google rewards engagement velocity.

### 3. Google Search Ads (Standard)
Second-tier to LSAs but still essential for trades where LSAs aren't available or for specific service searches ("tankless water heater installation" vs. generic "plumber near me"). Keep the radius tight: 10-15 miles for urban, 25-30 for suburban/rural.

## What Wastes Money

**Facebook/Instagram Ads:** Home services are searched, not scrolled. Nobody impulse-clicks "schedule an HVAC tune-up" while browsing Instagram. Social works for brand awareness in landscaping (visual portfolio) but the ROI for emergency/repair services is nearly zero. If you're spending here, stop.

**Yelp Ads:** Overpriced for the lead quality. Yelp's own organic results already surface businesses with good reviews. Paying for Yelp ads often means paying to appear above yourself. The cost-per-lead is 2-3x what you'd pay on Google.

**SEO-first strategies for new businesses:** If you have zero reviews and a 3-month-old website, SEO won't save you. It takes 6-12 months to rank for competitive local terms. Start with paid (LSAs + Search), earn reviews, and let organic build in the background.

## Budget Tiers

- **$500/mo:** All-in on LSAs. No split. Get leads flowing, earn reviews, build the GBP.
- **$2,000/mo:** LSAs ($1,200) + Google Search for specialty services ($500) + GBP optimization ($300 in time/tools).
- **$5,000/mo:** Multi-channel. LSAs ($2,000) + Search Ads ($1,500) + targeted direct mail for seasonal push ($1,000) + professional GBP management ($500). Consider a simple retargeting campaign for estimate-no-close follow-up.

## The One Mistake Everyone Makes

**Spreading budget across too many channels.** A plumber spending $200/month each on Google Ads, Facebook, Yelp, Thumbtack, and Angi is getting nothing from five channels instead of something from one. Consolidate to LSAs first. Add channels only after your primary channel is saturated and profitable.

## When Research Is Thin

If search returns little about the business, assume: they have few reviews (under 20), their GBP is incomplete, and they've probably tried boosting Facebook posts with disappointing results. Recommend LSAs as the starting point, GBP completion as the first week's homework, and ask whether they're getting leads from any source currently. The answer reveals whether this is a "turn on the faucet" problem or a "fix the plumbing first" problem.
