# SEO Fundamentals — Implementation Guide

## When to Recommend
As a secondary, long-term channel alongside paid acquisition. Never as the primary strategy for a business with zero organic traffic and no existing domain authority. Recommend at the $2,000+/month budget tier, or as "homework" at lower tiers (the owner does the work, not the agency). Most valuable for professional services (high-value keywords), content-driven e-commerce, and local businesses that can own neighborhood-specific terms.

## Setup Checklist (Month 1)
1. **Fix the technical basics.** Google Search Console set up and verified. Sitemap submitted. No broken pages (404s). Mobile-friendly (Google indexes mobile-first). Page speed under 3 seconds on mobile. These aren't "SEO tactics" — they're prerequisites. If the site is slow or broken, no amount of content helps.
2. **Claim and optimize Google Business Profile.** For local businesses, GBP IS the SEO strategy. A complete GBP outranks most website SEO work in local search results. (See the GBP channel file for details.)
3. **Identify 10-15 target keywords.** Not the obvious ones ("plumber," "lawyer"). The long-tail, specific ones where you can actually win: "emergency plumber Decatur GA," "ADHD testing for adults Greenwich CT," "organic vitamin C serum for sensitive skin." Use Google Search Console to see what you already rank for — often there are terms at positions 8-20 where a small push gets page-one visibility.
4. **Create one page per keyword cluster.** Each important service or topic gets its own page. A therapist shouldn't have one "Services" page — they should have separate pages for "ADHD Testing," "Anxiety Therapy," "Couples Counseling," each targeting specific search terms.
5. **Fix title tags and meta descriptions.** Every page needs a unique title tag (under 60 characters) that includes the target keyword and location. Meta descriptions (under 155 characters) should be compelling enough to click on — they're your ad copy in organic results.

## What to Do Monthly (Ongoing)
- **Publish 2-4 pieces of useful content.** Not "blog posts about your industry." Content that answers the questions your customers actually ask. A plumber writing "Why is my water heater making noise?" targets a real search query that leads to a service call. A therapist writing "Signs of ADHD in adult women" targets a real information need that leads to a consultation.
- **Build 2-5 backlinks per month.** Local business directories (Yelp, BBB, industry-specific), guest posts on relevant local or industry sites, and HARO (journalists looking for expert sources). Don't buy links — Google penalizes it. Quality over quantity.
- **Monitor rankings and traffic in Search Console.** Track the 10-15 target keywords monthly. Focus on keywords moving from page 2 to page 1 — that's where the biggest traffic jump happens.

## What Good Looks Like
- **Month 1-3:** Technical fixes done. Pages created. No significant traffic change yet. This is normal.
- **Month 3-6:** Some long-tail keywords hitting page 1. Traffic from organic should start growing 10-20% month-over-month. GBP appearing in more local searches.
- **Month 6-12:** Primary keywords approaching page 1. Organic traffic should be a meaningful percentage of total visitors (20-40%). Some pages ranking for keywords you didn't specifically target (semantic wins).
- **Month 12+:** Compounding returns. Content published 6 months ago continues driving traffic. Organic becomes the lowest cost-per-acquisition channel — but it took a year to get here.

## Common Mistakes the Plan Should Flag
- **Expecting results in 30 days.** SEO is a 6-12 month investment. If the business needs leads next month, start with paid. SEO builds in the background.
- **Writing content nobody searches for.** "Our team's favorite lunch spots" gets zero search traffic. "Best restaurants near [neighborhood]" gets thousands. Use keyword research, not editorial calendars.
- **Ignoring local SEO in favor of national terms.** A solo attorney trying to rank for "divorce lawyer" nationally is competing with Avvo, FindLaw, and LegalZoom. "Divorce lawyer [specific suburb]" is winnable.
- **Paying for SEO without clear deliverables.** "We'll optimize your site" is not a deliverable. Monthly content published, backlinks earned, and keyword ranking changes are measurable. The plan should set expectations about what SEO work actually looks like.

## Budget Guidance
- **$0/mo (owner-managed):** Fix technical basics yourself. Write one piece of content per month. Claim directories. Viable but slow.
- **$500-1,000/mo:** Professional SEO audit + monthly content creation (2-4 pieces) + basic link building. Results in 6-9 months.
- **$1,500-3,000/mo:** Comprehensive SEO program with technical optimization, content strategy, link building, and monthly reporting. Results in 4-6 months.
