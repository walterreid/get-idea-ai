# Google Search Ads — Implementation Guide

## When to Recommend
Any business where customers search for the service before buying. High-intent verticals: professional services ($15-80 CPC), home services ($8-25 CPC), fitness ($5-15 CPC), specialty restaurants ($3-10 CPC). Not primary for e-commerce (Google Shopping is better) or brand-awareness plays.

## Setup Checklist (Week 1)
1. **Start with 5-10 exact match keywords.** Not broad match — exact match. "[service] [city]" and "[specialty] near me" patterns. Example: "emergency plumber Decatur GA", "ADHD testing Greenwich CT". Broad match at low budgets burns money on irrelevant searches.
2. **Set a geographic radius.** 10-15 miles urban, 20-30 suburban. Don't target the whole state. The plan should specify the radius based on the business's actual service area.
3. **Write 3 responsive search ads.** Each ad needs 15 headlines and 4 descriptions. Pin the business name and primary service to headline positions 1-2. Include the city/neighborhood in at least 2 headlines.
4. **Set up conversion tracking.** Phone calls (minimum 60-second duration) and form submissions. Without conversion tracking, the algorithm optimizes for clicks, not leads. This is the most common setup failure.
5. **Add negative keywords from day one.** "free," "DIY," "jobs," "salary," "how to," "reddit." Review the search terms report weekly for the first month and add negatives aggressively.
6. **Send traffic to a dedicated landing page** — not the homepage. The page should match the ad's promise: service name, location, phone number above the fold, and a form. One page per service cluster.

## What Good Looks Like
- **Click-through rate (CTR):** 5-8% for service keywords is solid. Below 3% means the ad copy doesn't match intent.
- **Cost per lead:** Varies by vertical. Home services: $20-40. Professional services: $40-100. Fitness: $15-30. If CPL exceeds 10% of the customer's lifetime value, the math doesn't work.
- **Conversion rate (landing page):** 8-15% for high-intent service pages. Below 5% means the landing page is the problem, not the ads.
- **Quality Score:** 7+ on primary keywords. Below 6 means you're paying a penalty on every click. Fix ad relevance and landing page experience first.

## Common Mistakes the Plan Should Flag
- **Broad match keywords at low budget.** A plumber on $500/month with broad match "plumbing" will show for "plumbing salary," "DIY plumbing tips," and "plumbing school near me." Money gone.
- **Sending traffic to the homepage.** The homepage serves 10 audiences. A landing page serves one. Conversion rate doubles with a dedicated page.
- **No negative keywords.** The default search terms report is full of garbage. Weekly negative keyword reviews are non-optional for the first 90 days.
- **Optimizing for clicks instead of conversions.** Without conversion tracking, Google shows ads to people who click, not people who call. This is the difference between $30/lead and $90/lead.
- **Too many keywords, too little budget.** 50 keywords on $500/month means $10/month per keyword — not enough data to optimize anything. 5-10 keywords, deep investment, learn what converts.

## Budget Guidance
- **$500/mo minimum viable.** Enough for 5-8 keywords in a single service area. Expect 15-30 leads/month for home services, 5-12 for professional services.
- **$1,000-2,000/mo:** Add specialty service keywords, expand to 2-3 service clusters. Start A/B testing ad copy and landing pages.
- **$3,000+/mo:** Multiple campaigns per service line. Automated bidding (Target CPA) becomes viable with enough conversion data (30+ conversions/month).
