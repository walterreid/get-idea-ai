# Google Business Profile (GBP) — Implementation Guide

## When to Recommend
Every business with a physical location or service area. Non-negotiable for local services, restaurants, fitness, and local professional services. Not relevant for pure e-commerce/DTC.

## Setup Checklist (Week 1)
1. **Claim or create the listing** at business.google.com. If already claimed, verify the owner has admin access — many businesses have listings managed by a former agency or an employee who left.
2. **Complete every field.** Business name (exact legal name, no keyword stuffing — Google penalizes it). Primary category (most specific available). Secondary categories (up to 9). Hours, phone, website, service area or address.
3. **Add 20+ photos.** Exterior (helps Maps recognition), interior, team, and work samples. Real photos from a phone outperform stock. Update monthly.
4. **Write the business description.** 750 characters. Lead with what makes this business different, not a list of services. Include the city/neighborhood naturally.
5. **Add services or menu.** Google shows these in search results. Each service listed is a potential search match.
6. **Turn on messaging** if the business can respond within 4 hours. Turn it off if they can't — slow responses hurt ranking.

## What "Weekly Activity" Means
- **Post once per week** using Google Posts. Job completed photos, seasonal offers, team updates. Posts expire after 7 days — consistency matters more than polish.
- **Respond to every review within 24 hours.** Positive reviews: thank specifically ("Glad the kitchen remodel turned out great"). Negative reviews: acknowledge, don't argue, offer to resolve offline. Response rate and speed affect ranking.
- **Add new photos weekly.** The GBP algorithm rewards freshness. One photo per week from actual work beats a quarterly photoshoot.
- **Answer questions in the Q&A section.** Seed it with 5-10 common questions yourself — competitors and random people will answer them otherwise.

## What Good Looks Like
- **50+ reviews** with a 4.2+ average: competitive in most local markets
- **100+ reviews**: dominant in small/mid markets, competitive in dense urban
- **3-pack appearance**: showing up in the Maps results for your primary service + neighborhood. This is the goal — it's free traffic from high-intent searches.
- **Insights metrics**: track "searches" (how often you appear) and "actions" (calls, website clicks, directions). Actions should be 5-15% of searches.

## Common Mistakes the Plan Should Flag
- **Keyword-stuffed business name.** "Joe's Plumbing | Emergency Plumber | 24/7 Plumbing Service NYC" violates Google's guidelines and risks suspension. Use the real business name.
- **Wrong primary category.** A therapist listed as "Counselor" when "Psychologist" matches their license. Category determines which searches trigger the listing.
- **Ignoring negative reviews.** Unanswered negative reviews signal to Google (and customers) that the business doesn't care. A thoughtful response to a 1-star review can convert a reader better than five 5-star reviews.
- **Set-and-forget.** A GBP with no posts, no new photos, and no review responses in 6 months is invisible to Google's freshness algorithm.

## Budget Guidance
- **$0/mo (owner-managed):** Viable if the owner commits 30 minutes/week. Post, respond to reviews, add photos.
- **$200-400/mo (managed):** Professional GBP management service handles posts, review responses, photo uploads, and Q&A monitoring. Worth it for owners who won't do it themselves.
