# Email & SMS Marketing — Implementation Guide

## When to Recommend
Every business with repeat customers or a sales cycle longer than one visit. Critical for e-commerce (25-40% of revenue should come from email). High-value for restaurants (repeat visit activation), fitness (member retention and win-back), and professional services (referral nurturing). Less impactful for one-time home services (you don't email your plumber monthly) — but review request sequences still matter.

## Setup Checklist (Week 1-2)
1. **Choose a platform.** At $500/mo total marketing budget: Mailchimp (free tier, up to 500 contacts) or MailerLite (free tier, up to 1,000). At $1,000+: Klaviyo for e-commerce (best Shopify integration, $20/mo for 500 contacts), or ActiveCampaign for services ($29/mo, best automation for non-e-commerce). Don't overcomplicate this — the platform matters less than actually sending emails.
2. **Build the first flow before sending any campaign.** A flow is an automated sequence triggered by an action. Start with the one flow that matters most for the business type (see below).
3. **Import existing contacts.** POS system exports (restaurants), membership database (fitness), client list (professional services), customer list (e-commerce). Segment immediately: active customers vs. lapsed vs. leads.
4. **Set up a signup mechanism.** Website popup (10% discount for e-commerce, "first month free" for fitness, "free consultation" for services). Collect email + first name minimum. Add SMS opt-in checkbox — SMS has 98% open rates vs. 20% for email.

## The Flows That Matter (by Business Type)

**E-commerce — build these four:**
1. **Welcome series (5 emails over 10 days).** Email 1: discount code + brand story. Email 2: bestsellers. Email 3: social proof/reviews. Email 4: founder story or mission. Email 5: urgency on the discount expiring.
2. **Abandoned cart (3 emails + 1 SMS).** 1 hour: "You left something behind" + product image. 24 hours: social proof for the product. 48 hours: discount or free shipping. SMS at 2 hours if opted in.
3. **Post-purchase.** Day 2: order confirmation + what to expect. Day 7: "How's your [product]?" Day 14: review request. Day 30: cross-sell related product.
4. **Win-back (90-day lapsed).** "We miss you" + discount. One reminder. Then suppress — don't keep emailing people who don't want to hear from you.

**Restaurants:**
1. **Post-visit follow-up.** If POS captures email: "Thanks for dining with us" + review request link. Simple, huge impact.
2. **Monthly newsletter.** New menu items, upcoming events, chef's picks. Keep it short — 3 items max. Link to reservation page.

**Fitness:**
1. **Welcome series.** Class schedule, what to expect, member spotlight. Reduce no-shows by setting expectations.
2. **Win-back (30-day inactive).** "We noticed you haven't been in — here's a free guest pass for a friend." Reactivation, not guilt.

**Professional Services:**
1. **Nurture sequence for leads.** After initial consultation: "Here's what we discussed" + next steps. Week 2: relevant case study. Week 4: "Ready to move forward?" No pressure — the sequence builds trust.

## What Good Looks Like
- **Open rate:** 30-45% for flows (automated), 18-25% for campaigns (broadcasts). Below 15% means deliverability issues or a stale list.
- **Click rate:** 3-5% for flows, 1.5-3% for campaigns.
- **Revenue attribution (e-commerce):** 25-40% of total revenue from email/SMS is healthy. Under 15% means the flows aren't built or the list is too small.
- **List growth:** 100-300 new subscribers/month for a small business is solid. If growth is flat, the signup mechanism needs work.

## Common Mistakes the Plan Should Flag
- **Sending campaigns without flows.** Campaigns are broadcasts to the whole list. Flows are targeted sequences triggered by behavior. Flows convert 3-5x better. Build flows first, then add campaigns.
- **Buying email lists.** Destroys deliverability. Every email should be earned through opt-in. No exceptions.
- **Emailing too frequently without value.** For most SMBs, once per week is the ceiling for campaigns. More than that requires genuinely valuable content (not "20% off everything, again").
- **No SMS.** SMS has 98% open rates and 10-15% click rates. For time-sensitive offers (flash sales, event reminders, appointment confirmations), SMS dramatically outperforms email.

## Budget Guidance
- **$0-50/mo:** Free tier platforms. Owner manages. Build one automated flow and send one campaign per month.
- **$200-500/mo:** Paid platform with automation. 3-4 flows running. Weekly campaigns. Professional templates.
- **$500-1,000/mo:** Full email/SMS program with advanced segmentation, A/B testing, and monthly reporting. Usually requires a specialist or agency.
