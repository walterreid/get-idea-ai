# LinkedIn (Organic + Ads) — Implementation Guide

## When to Recommend
B2B professional services only: consultants, accountants, business attorneys, financial advisors, agencies, and B2B SaaS. The decision-maker must be on LinkedIn for this to work. Not for therapists, personal injury lawyers, restaurants, fitness, retail, or consumer e-commerce — the audience isn't there.

## Organic LinkedIn — Setup and Cadence

### Week 1 Setup
1. **Optimize the personal profile** (not the company page). On LinkedIn, people follow people. The owner's profile is the asset. Headline should state who they help and what outcome they deliver: "I help mid-market CFOs cut audit costs by 30%" not "Managing Partner at Smith & Associates."
2. **Write a Featured section.** Pin 1-2 posts that demonstrate expertise. A case study, a contrarian take on the industry, or a "here's what I told a client this week" story.
3. **Connect with 50-100 ideal prospects.** Not spray-and-pray. Targeted: people in the role you sell to, in the industries you serve, in the geography you cover. No pitch in the connection request — just connect.

### Posting Cadence
- **3 posts per week.** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday between 7-9am local time. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards consistency over volume.
- **Content that works:** Short posts (150-300 words). Start with a hook that creates tension ("Most consultants price their services wrong. Here's why."). Share a specific judgment call from real client work (anonymized). End with a question or a clear takeaway. No hashtag spam — 3 relevant hashtags maximum.
- **Content that doesn't work:** Company announcements ("We're excited to announce..."), motivational quotes, long articles (algorithm buries them), and anything that reads like a press release.

### Engagement Strategy
- **Comment on 5-10 posts per day** from people in your target audience. Substantive comments — add an insight, disagree respectfully, share a related experience. This puts your name in front of prospects more reliably than posting alone.
- **Reply to every comment on your posts** within 2 hours. The algorithm boosts posts with active comment threads.

## LinkedIn Ads — When They Justify the CPM

### When to Use
Only when: (1) the client value is $5,000+ per engagement, (2) the audience is narrowly definable by job title/company size/industry, and (3) the organic strategy is already generating engagement (proof the audience responds to the messaging).

LinkedIn Ads CPM is $30-80 — 5-10x more expensive than Meta. The math only works for high-value B2B.

### Campaign Setup
1. **Objective: Lead Generation** (LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms). Not website traffic. Lead Gen Forms have 2-5x the conversion rate of landing pages because users don't leave LinkedIn.
2. **Audience: Job title + company size + industry.** Start narrow: "CFOs at companies with 50-500 employees in manufacturing." Don't target by interest or group — too broad.
3. **Creative: Single image ads** with a clear value proposition. "The 3 tax strategies mid-market manufacturers miss every year — free guide." Offer a lead magnet, not a sales pitch.
4. **Budget: $50/day minimum** ($1,500/month). Below this, LinkedIn doesn't generate enough impressions to optimize.

### What Good Looks Like
- **Cost per lead (Lead Gen Forms):** $30-75 for B2B professional services. Above $100 means the targeting or offer needs work.
- **Lead form completion rate:** 10-15% of clicks. Below 5% means the form has too many fields or the offer isn't compelling.
- **Pipeline conversion:** 5-15% of LinkedIn leads should convert to a meeting. If meetings don't happen, the lead magnet attracted the wrong people.

## Common Mistakes the Plan Should Flag
- **Running LinkedIn Ads without organic foundation.** If someone clicks your ad and visits a profile with zero posts, they bounce. Build the organic presence first (4-6 weeks minimum), then amplify with ads.
- **Company page content instead of personal content.** Company pages get 2-5% the organic reach of personal profiles. The founder/principal should be the voice, not the brand.
- **Selling in DMs immediately after connecting.** The fastest way to get blocked. Build visibility through content first. DM only after they've engaged with your posts 2-3 times.
- **Targeting too broadly.** "Marketing professionals" is millions of people. "CMOs at B2B SaaS companies with 100-1,000 employees in the US" is a targetable audience. Narrow wins.

## Budget Guidance
- **$0/mo (organic only):** 3 posts/week + daily commenting. Viable for consultants willing to invest 3-5 hours/week. Results in 8-12 weeks.
- **$1,500-3,000/mo (organic + ads):** Organic foundation plus Lead Gen Form campaigns targeting narrow B2B audience. Generates 20-60 leads/month depending on targeting.
