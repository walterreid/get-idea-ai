# Google Local Services Ads (LSAs) — Implementation Guide

## When to Recommend
Home services businesses: plumbing, HVAC, electricians, locksmiths, roofing, garage door, pest control, cleaning, landscaping, and similar trades. Also available for some professional services (lawyers, financial planners, real estate agents) in select markets. Not available for restaurants, retail, fitness, or e-commerce.

## Setup Checklist (Week 1-2)
1. **Apply for Google Guaranteed badge.** This requires: business license verification, insurance verification (general liability minimum), and a background check on the owner. The background check takes 2-5 business days. No badge = no LSAs.
2. **Set your service area.** Zip codes, not radius. Be specific — LSAs charge per lead, so every zip code you add is leads you'll pay for. Start tight: the 5-10 zip codes where you actually want to work.
3. **Choose your service categories.** Each category is a separate lead stream. Start with your highest-margin service. A plumber might start with "drain cleaning" and "water heater repair" — not "general plumbing" which attracts low-value calls.
4. **Set your weekly budget.** LSAs use a weekly budget, not monthly. $150/week ($600/month) is a reasonable starting point for one trade in one market. Google will try to spend the full budget — set it conservatively and increase after you see lead quality.
5. **Set business hours for lead delivery.** LSAs only show during hours you've marked as available. If you can take calls 7am-9pm, set that. Narrower hours = fewer leads but higher answer rate.
6. **Upload a professional headshot.** LSAs display the business owner's photo. A clear headshot builds trust. No logos, no team photos — one face.

## How LSA Leads Work
- **Leads arrive as phone calls or messages** directly through the Google interface. The customer sees your business name, photo, Google Guaranteed badge, review rating, and a "Call" button.
- **You pay per lead, not per click.** A lead is a phone call you answer or a message you respond to. Average cost: $15-50 depending on trade and market. Plumbing emergency calls are at the high end; cleaning services at the low end.
- **You can dispute invalid leads.** Spam calls, wrong-service requests, and calls from outside your area can be disputed within 30 days. Google credits about 20-30% of disputed leads. Dispute everything that's not a real customer inquiry — many businesses leave money on the table here.

## What Good Looks Like
- **Answer rate above 80%.** LSAs penalize businesses that don't answer. If a lead calls and goes to voicemail, your ranking drops. Have someone answering the phone during business hours.
- **15-25% booking rate.** Of the leads that call, 15-25% should become booked jobs. Below 10% means either lead quality is poor (wrong categories) or the phone process needs work.
- **Cost per acquisition:** Lead cost × (1 / booking rate). A $30 lead with 20% booking = $150 per customer. If the average job is $300+, the math works.
- **Review growth.** After completing a job, Google prompts the customer to leave a review on the LSA profile. This is the fastest review engine available. 5+ new reviews per month is normal for an active LSA account.

## Common Mistakes the Plan Should Flag
- **Too many zip codes.** Covering a 50-mile radius means paying for leads you'll drive 45 minutes to reach. Start with the 5-10 closest zip codes.
- **Not disputing leads.** Every spam call or wrong-number lead you don't dispute is $20-40 wasted. Dispute weekly.
- **Broad service categories.** "General plumbing" generates volume but low intent. "Water heater repair" and "drain cleaning" generate callers who know what they need and are ready to book.
- **No one answers the phone.** The #1 LSA failure mode. Google's ranking algorithm heavily penalizes missed calls. If the owner can't answer, hire an answering service ($100-200/month) before spending on LSAs.

## Budget Guidance
- **$500/mo ($125/week):** 10-20 leads/month depending on market. Enough to test and learn which categories convert.
- **$1,500-2,000/mo:** Saturates most single-trade local markets. Beyond this, lead quality drops as Google reaches further from your core area.
- **$3,000+/mo:** Only justified for multi-trade (plumbing + HVAC) or multi-location. Diminishing returns for single-trade in a single market.
